Abstract

[cite_start]Variational quantum circuits (VQCs) offer compact, trainable models that may reduce parameter counts and improve stability for reinforcement learning (RL) in the noisy intermediate-scale quantum (NISQ) era[cite: 5]. [cite_start]However, performance depends strongly on design choices in encoding, ansatz, and readout[cite: 5]. [cite_start]This paper proposes Differentiable Quantum Architecture Co-Search (DQACS), a fully differentiable framework that jointly optimizes variational gate placements together with trainable encoding maps and readout layers[cite: 6]. [cite_start]DQACS is integrated into a hybrid quantum-classical PPO pipeline with framestacking and a top-k selection schedule to improve training stability[cite: 7]. [cite_start]Experiments show the method learns diverse environments and convergence improves as the search collapses to a high-probability architecture[cite: 8].
